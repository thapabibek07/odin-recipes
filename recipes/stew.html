Thor's Hearty Stew

Thor's s Hearty Stew is a fictional, hearty meal you might imagine a Norse god like Thor enjoying after a long day of battling giants and wielding Mjölnir. This stew would be rich in flavor, robust with root vegetables, and packed with hearty meats like beef or lamb. It would be a satisfying meal, perfect for refueling after an epic adventure.


Thor's Hearty Stew Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs beef stew meat (or lamb for a more traditional touch)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil (or butter)
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 4 large carrots, peeled and sliced into thick rounds
  • 4 medium potatoes, peeled and cut into cubes
  • 2 parsnips, peeled and sliced
  • 2 stalks celery, chopped
  • 2 cups beef Broth (or stock)
  • 1 cup red wine (optional, for richness)
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp dried rosemary
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 cup peas (frozen or fresh)
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Meat:
    • Heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil in a large, heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat.
    • Season the beef (or lamb) with salt and pepper. Brown the meat in batches to avoid overcrowding. Once browned, remove it from the pot and set it aside.
  2. Sauté Aromatics:
    • In the same pot, add another tablespoon of oil (or butter).
    • Add the chopped onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
    • Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  3. Build the Stew:
    • Stir in the tomato paste and let it cook for 1-2 minutes to deepen its flavor.
    • Return the browned meat to the pot.
    • Add the carrots, potatoes, parsnips, and celery. Stir everything together.
  4. Add Liquids & Seasoning:
    • Pour in the beef broth and red wine (if using). Stir to combine, scraping up any brown bits from the bottom of the pot.
    • Add the bay leaves, thyme, and rosemary. Season with salt and pepper.
    • Bring the stew to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot and let the stew simmer for 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the meat is tender and the vegetables are cooked through.
  5. Finish the Stew:
    • About 10 minutes before serving, stir in the peas and cook until tender.
    • Taste and adjust seasoning with more salt, pepper, or herbs if needed.
  6. Serve:
    • Ladle the stew into bowls and garnish with fresh chopped parsley.
    • Serve with crusty bread to soak up the delicious broth.

This stew is perfect for a cold winter’s day and will definitely fill you with the strength of Thor himself!
